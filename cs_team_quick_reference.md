# Customer Success Team Quick Reference Guide

## Daily Checklist

### Morning (First 30 minutes)
- [ ] Check health dashboard for at-risk customers (Red/Yellow)
- [ ] Review overnight support tickets
- [ ] Check calendar for today's customer meetings
- [ ] Review daily metrics from previous day

### Throughout the Day
- [ ] Respond to customer emails within 4 hours
- [ ] Log all customer interactions in CRM
- [ ] Follow up on action items from previous meetings
- [ ] Monitor community forum for urgent questions

### End of Day (Last 30 minutes)
- [ ] Update customer health scores
- [ ] Schedule follow-ups for next day
- [ ] Document lessons learned
- [ ] Plan priorities for tomorrow

## Customer Health Tiers - Immediate Actions

### 🟢 Green (80-100)
**Characteristics:** High usage, engaged, satisfied
**Actions:**
- Monthly check-in email
- Share success stories
- Invite to referral program
- Feature adoption suggestions

### 🟡 Yellow (60-79)
**Characteristics:** Moderate usage, some engagement issues
**Actions:**
- Weekly check-in call
- Usage optimization suggestions
- Feature training offer
- Success plan review

### 🔴 Red (0-59)
**Characteristics:** Low usage, disengaged, dissatisfied
**Actions:**
- Immediate personal outreach
- Root cause analysis
- Recovery plan development
- Executive escalation if needed

## Support Response Times

### Priority Levels:
- **P1 (Critical):** System down, data loss → 1 hour response
- **P2 (High):** Feature broken, security issue → 4 hours response
- **P3 (Medium):** Configuration help, bugs → 24 hours response
- **P4 (Low):** Feature requests, documentation → 72 hours response

### First Response Guidelines:
- Acknowledge receipt immediately
- Set clear expectations for resolution time
- Provide regular updates if resolution takes time
- Follow up after resolution to ensure satisfaction

## Customer Communication Templates

### Quick Email Responses:

**For feature requests:**
"Thanks for the suggestion! I've added it to our product roadmap for consideration. I'll keep you updated on progress."

**For bug reports:**
"I've reproduced the issue and our engineering team is investigating. I'll provide an update by [time/date]."

**For configuration help:**
"Let me help you with that. Here's a step-by-step guide: [link]. Would a quick screenshare help?"

**For billing questions:**
"I've reviewed your account and here are the details: [specifics]. Let me know if you need anything else."

### Meeting Agendas:

**15-Minute Check-in:**
1. Quick wins (2 min)
2. Current challenges (5 min)
3. Optimization opportunities (5 min)
4. Next steps (3 min)

**30-Minute Quarterly Review:**
1. Previous quarter achievements (5 min)
2. Current metrics review (10 min)
3. Next quarter goals (10 min)
4. Action items (5 min)

## Key Metrics to Know

### Daily/Monthly Targets:
- **Health Score Average:** > 75
- **CSAT Score:** > 4.5/5
- **First Response Time:** < 4 hours
- **Resolution Time:** < 24 hours (P3)
- **Customer Logins:** > 3/week
- **Feature Adoption:** > 60% of available features

### Warning Signs:
- Health score drop > 20 points
- No logins for 14 days
- > 5 support tickets in 7 days
- Failed payment attempt
- Negative NPS/CSAT feedback

## Escalation Path

### When to Escalate:
1. **To Senior CSM:** Complex account issues, strategic discussions
2. **To Engineering:** Critical bugs, system outages
3. **To Leadership:** At-risk key accounts, strategic partnerships
4. **To Executive:** Critical churn risk, major account issues

### Escalation Template:
"Escalating [customer] for [reason]. Current status: [brief]. Required action: [specific]. Timeline: [urgency]."
